# About Dativo — Building compliance infrastructure for connected products

Dativo exists to make EU Data Act compliance operationally feasible for every connected product manufacturer — from a 50-person OEM running Docker Compose to a €5B industrial group deploying on Kubernetes.

## Our Mission

The EU Data Act (Regulation (EU) 2023/2854) represents a fundamental shift in how manufacturers must handle the data generated by their connected products. Since September 2025, customers have a legal right to access the data produced by the products they purchase, and to share it with third parties of their choice.

This creates a complex operational challenge: manufacturers must build infrastructure to register equipment, map data sources, classify trade secrets, and fulfill data access requests — continuously, for every customer, every product line, and every new data source. Doing this manually doesn't scale.

Dativo exists to solve this problem. We provide the open-core platform that makes EU Data Act compliance operational, auditable, and scalable — so manufacturers can focus on building great products.

## Our Principles

### Built by engineers who use AI to move fast
We ship in weeks what others plan for quarters. Our team embraces AI as a development accelerator — not just a product feature, but a way we build. The result is faster iteration and higher quality.

### Open Source Commitment
We believe the compliance engine should be transparent and auditable. The Community Edition is genuinely useful — not a lead gen trap. The commercial features exist because enterprise compliance, advanced integrations, and managed infrastructure are genuinely harder problems worth paying for.

### Regulatory Depth
We don't build generic data tools. Every feature is designed around specific Data Act articles and their practical implications for manufacturers.

### Industrial Focus
We understand manufacturing environments — the data volumes, the integration complexity, the deployment constraints. CISOs at industrial OEMs won't send equipment telemetry to third-party clouds. That's why we offer self-hosted deployment with source transparency.
